Friends - The TV Show

Friendsis an American television sitcom created by David Crane and Marta Kauffman, which aired on NBC from September 22, 1994, to May 6, 2004, lasting ten seasons.
The show ranked within the top ten of the final television season ratings; it ultimately reached the number-one spot in its eighth season. The series finale aired on May 6, 2004, and was watched by around 52.5 million American viewers, making it the fifth-most-watched series finale in television history

Casts

(From left to right)

  • Matthew Perry

    - as Chandler Bing

    Chandler is known for his sarcastic sense of humor. He works in a corporate office job and later becomes a statistical analysis and data reconfiguration specialist.

  • Jennifer Aniston

    - as Rachel Green

    Rachel is a fashion-conscious woman who starts the series by running away from her wedding. She moves in with her old friend Monica and works at Central Perk as a waitress. Over the course of the series, she explores various career paths, including working in the fashion industry

  • David Schwimmer

    - as Ross Geller

    Ross is Monica's brother and a paleontologist with a passion for dinosaurs. He's known for his on-again, off-again relationship with Rachel and his three marriages throughout the series.

  • Courteney Cox

    - as Monica Geller

    Monica is Rachel's best friend and is known for her obsessive-compulsive tendencies. She is a chef and is responsible for maintaining the cleanliness and organization of the group's apartment.

  • Matt LeBlanc

    - as Joey Tribbiani

    Joey is a lovable but not very bright aspiring actor who becomes a close friend to the others. He's known for his catchphrase "How you doin'?" and his role in a soap opera called "Days of Our Lives."

  • Lisa Kudrow

    - as Phoebe Buffay

    Phoebe is an eccentric and somewhat mystical character with a tragic past. She's a musician who plays the guitar and writes quirky, humorous songs. She also works as a masseuse.

These six main characters form the core of "Friends," and their interactions and relationships are the central focus of the show. The series explores their personal and professional lives, romantic entanglements, and the challenges and adventures they face while navigating the ups and downs of adulthood in New York City.
